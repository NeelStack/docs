# NeelStack Engineering KPIs

> **"What gets measured gets improved. What gets improved compounds into engineering excellence."**

---

# Executive Summary

Engineering organizations should not rely on intuition to evaluate performance.

High-performing engineering organizations measure engineering health using objective, repeatable, and actionable metrics.

The purpose of Engineering KPIs is **not** to monitor individuals.

The purpose is to continuously improve:

- Engineering Quality
- Product Quality
- Customer Satisfaction
- Platform Maturity
- Developer Experience
- Operational Excellence

Engineering KPIs should support learning, continuous improvement, and informed decision-making.

They should never encourage unhealthy competition or metric manipulation.

---

# Purpose

Engineering KPIs enable NeelStack to:

- Measure engineering effectiveness.
- Identify bottlenecks.
- Improve delivery.
- Increase quality.
- Reduce operational risk.
- Improve customer satisfaction.
- Guide engineering investments.

Every KPI should influence meaningful engineering improvement.

---

# KPI Principles

Every Engineering KPI must satisfy the following principles.

## Objective

Metrics should rely on measurable data.

---

## Actionable

Teams should know how to improve the metric.

---

## Transparent

Every engineer should understand how metrics are calculated.

---

## Comparable

Metrics should allow comparison over time.

---

## Balanced

No single KPI defines engineering success.

Engineering performance is evaluated across multiple dimensions.

# KPI Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- Measuring individuals instead of systems.
- Optimizing one KPI while harming others.
- Using KPIs as punishment.
- Tracking metrics without action.
- Selecting metrics that cannot influence decisions.

Healthy engineering organizations use KPIs for learning.

# Engineering Scorecard

Engineering KPIs become actionable when presented through a consistent scorecard.

The Engineering Scorecard provides a standardized view of engineering health across products, teams, and the organization.

The scorecard should prioritize trends over isolated measurements.

---

# Scorecard Objectives

The Engineering Scorecard exists to:

- Measure engineering health
- Identify improvement opportunities
- Track long-term progress
- Support executive decisions
- Encourage continuous improvement
- Maintain engineering transparency

The scorecard is not a performance review tool for individuals.


# SRE Metrics

Site Reliability Engineering introduces service-focused metrics.

---

## Service Level Indicator (SLI)

Measures actual service performance.

Examples

- Availability
- Latency
- Error Rate
- Throughput

---

## Service Level Objective (SLO)

Defines expected service performance.

Example

Availability

99.95%

---

## Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Customer-facing commitment.

Example

99.9% uptime.

SLAs should always be supported by internal SLOs.

# Closing Statement

Engineering excellence cannot be sustained without measurement.

However, measurement alone is insufficient.

Metrics must inspire learning, encourage collaboration, guide investment, and strengthen engineering systems.

The objective of Engineering KPIs is not to optimize numbers.

The objective is to build an engineering organization capable of continuously delivering exceptional products through disciplined engineering, data-driven improvement, and operational excellence.
