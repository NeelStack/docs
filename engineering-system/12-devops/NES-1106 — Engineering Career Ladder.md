# NES-1106 — Engineering Career Ladder

> **"Career progression must be transparent and objective. We define clear career tracks, level expectations, and assessment criteria."**

---

# Executive Summary

Evaluating developer progression without transparent, documented guidelines leads to subjective promotions, team misalignment, and high employee turnover.

We mandate the adoption of the **NeelStack Engineering Career Ladder**.

This standard establishes our career tracks (Individual Contributor vs. Management), role level expectations, and performance evaluation criteria.

---

# Purpose

This standard defines:

- Dual Career Tracks (IC and Management)
- Role Levels and Expectations (L1 to L6)
- Impact, Leadership, and Technical Competencies
- Performance Review Cycles
- Promotion Validation Procedures

---

# Dual Career Tracks

We provide two parallel career tracks, allowing engineers to grow without being forced into management roles:

- **Individual Contributor (IC) Track**: Focuses on technical execution, system architecture, security, and data platforms design.
- **Management Track**: Focuses on team leadership, project delivery, people management, and organizational alignment.

---

# Role Levels & Technical Expectations

We define six engineering levels across the Individual Contributor track:

| Level | Role Title | Technical Expectation | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L1** | **Associate Engineer** | Writes basic code, requires guidance on architecture. | Local Tasks |
| **L2** | **Engineer** | Owns features, writes unit tests, participates in reviews. | Feature |
| **L3** | **Senior Engineer** | Designs modules, refactors tech debt, guides juniors. | Module |
| **L4** | **Staff Engineer** | Architectures multi-service systems, reviews ADRs. | System |
| **L5** | **Principal Engineer** | Leads platform tech strategies, resolves organizational risks. | Division |
| **L6** | **Distinguished Eng** | Directs company-wide technology vision and compliance. | Enterprise |

---

# Core Competency Dimensions

Promotion evaluations assess engineers across four primary competency dimensions:

1. **Technology & Code**: Technical execution, code quality, unit test discipline, and system performance.
2. **Delivery & Ownership**: Project tracking, task estimation, bug remediation, and SLA compliance.
3. **Leadership & Influence**: Mentorship of junior staff, cross-team collaboration, and code review quality.
4. **Business & Compliance**: Understanding customer requirements, data security compliance, and strategic alignment.

---

# Promotion Review Workflow

Ensure objective promotions:

- **Performance Reviews**: Conduct formal evaluations every **6 months** based on competency rubrics.
- **Promotion Panel**: Promotions to Staff Engineer (L4) and above require review by the **Engineering Promotion Panel** to verify the candidate meets organizational standards.
- **Evidence portfolio**: Candidates compile an evidence portfolio (commits, ADRs, design docs, certifications) to document impact.

---

# Anti-Patterns

❌ **Accidental Managers**: Forcing senior developers into management roles because it is the only way to get a pay raise, losing tech talent.

❌ **Vague Expectations**: Promoting engineers based on subjective manager opinions or tenure instead of objective competency achievements.

❌ **Excluding Soft Skills**: Evaluating only code volumes while ignoring collaboration, code review quality, or documentation support.

# Success Criteria

The Career Ladder program is successful when:
- Developers have a transparent, documented path for career growth.
- Promotion decisions are objective, repeatable, and audit-ready.
- Engineering staff retention metrics remain high.
